About

Hello! I am a PhD student in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at S V National Institute of Technology, Surat, India. I work at the Advanced Fluid Dynamics Lab (AFDL) headed by Dr. Jyotirmay Banerjee. AFDL includes projects ranging from experimental analysis of two-phase flow in opaque and transperent pipes to the numerical analysis of various fluid instabilities.

Presently, I am numerically analysing the spatially oscillating liquid jets. In practical applications these jets are formed using the fluidic oscillators. These jets also known as sweeping jets are essential in the variety of industrial applications that require uniform coating, thorough cleaning, efficient combustion, cooling/heating, atomization, flow control etc. Thus, it is essential to understand such jets for their inherent instabilities and behaviour under different flow conditions. Therefore, presently I carry out the numerical analysis which is performed based on the in-house developed high fidelity code that solves Navier-Stokes equations that are coupled with volume-of-fluid (VOF) equation to track the gas-liquid interface.

Research

My research is primarily oriented towards the numerical analysis of the spatially oscillating planar liquid jet. The research was undertaken to understand the instability mechanism in such jets and their behaviour under the different flow conditions. The applications such as flow control, cooling/heating the desired system, combustion etc. require precise design and understanding of such jets. The intricate details about such jets become even more important due to their superior performance over the steady jets in these applications. Therefore, the present study provides the fundamental understanding of these jets so that industrial equipments using these jets can be designed in a better manner.

*Our study till date has revealed the following aspects of the oscillating jets:

i) The reason of growth in amplitude of jet oscillations:
The numerical results showed that the jet becomes unstable due to the low pressure region cretaed at every oscillation peak by the adjacent vortex structures. This growth in amplitude is exponential and is affected by the fluid properties and flow conditions.

ii) The mechanism of jet breakup:
It is found that accompanying Kelvin-Helmholtz (KH) instability the instabilities such as Rayleigh-Taylor (RT) and Rayleigh-Plateau (RP) are also responsible for liquid breakup.

iii) Effect of viscosity, surface tension, inlet velocity profile and gravity on the jet:
It is found through parametric study that surface tension and viscosity stabilize the perturbations in an oscillating planar jet. On the other hand, increase in Froude number (Fr) initially leads to an augmentation of perturbation amplitude and later causes its damping when surface tension forces become dominant. The numerical analysis for different inlet velocity profiles establishes that the jet is more stable when subjected to parabolic inlet velocity profile as compared to uniform profile due to lower relative velocity at the interface.

I have written the codes in C++ using finite volume method to solve Navier-Stokes equations and the files will be available for download here. If there are any queries regarding the code please feel free to contact me.

  • 2D Lid-driven Cavity problem : code download

    The code is using a blended advection scheme.The NS solver is semi-explicit where velocity is solved explicitly whereas, pressure is obtained implicitly. The code is made to run parallel using OpenMP API.

    The code solves the Volume-of-Fluid equation geometrically using PLIC reconstruction. The VOF equation is made second order accurate by using operator-split method.

  • Dam-Break Test Case: code download

    The code solves the Navier-Stokes equations which are coupled with PLIC-VOF solver. The test case is based on Martin et al. paper.

  • Algebraic VOF (SAISH VOF scheme): code download

    The code solves volume fraction equation using a novel compressive interrface capturing scheme called "Smoothly Adapting Interfacial Scheme based on Hybridization" (SAISH). The code also involves the option to solve the VOF equation using other algebraic VOF methods such as CICSAM, M-CICSAM, CUIBS and FBICS.
